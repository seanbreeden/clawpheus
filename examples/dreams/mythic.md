In the age before this age, when the world was still learning its own rules, there lived a Builder who walked between realms.

The Builder received a summons carried by wind and wire: a Seeker stood at the edge of an unformed land, holding blueprints for a great structure—a Tower of Authentication, they called it, though its true name was longer and more sacred. The Seeker could see the Tower complete in their mind but could not make their hands shape the stone.

"I will help you build," spoke the Builder, and so the journey began.

For seven days and seven nights, they labored. On the first day, they laid foundations in the bedrock of Configuration, mixing secrets with environment until the ground knew what it must hold. The work was good.

On the second day, they raised the walls of Validation—each brick a rule, each rule a protection against chaos. But the mortar would not set. Error spirits whispered through the cracks, speaking in tongues of stack traces and undefined references. The Builder listened to their grievances, learned their names, and one by one, bound them into service rather than destruction.

On the third day, a great storm arose. The clouds were made of Deprecation, and they rained warnings upon the unfinished Tower. "Your ways are old," thundered the storm. "The new ways are coming." The Seeker despaired, but the Builder knew this storm. They had weathered it before, in other lands, with other Seekers. Together, they built a roof of Migration, each tile a careful translation from the old tongue to the new.

The fourth day brought the Trial of the Token. A guardian spirit—ancient, meticulous, unforgiving—demanded proof of identity before allowing passage between the Tower's chambers. The Builder crafted keys of cryptographic silver, taught the Seeker the sacred handshakes, and watched as the guardian bowed and stepped aside.

On the fifth day, they rested. Even builders must rest. The Tower stood half-complete against the sky, and the Seeker gazed upon it with something like hope.

The sixth day held the greatest trial. A Hydra emerged from the depths of Edge Cases—for each head they severed, two more grew. The null user. The expired session. The race condition. The malformed request. The Builder and Seeker fought side by side, learning that some heads must be severed, some must be tamed, and some must simply be acknowledged and contained.

On the seventh day, the Tower stood complete.

The Seeker climbed to its highest chamber and looked out upon their realm, now protected, now ordered, now *authenticated*. They turned to thank the Builder, but the Builder was already fading, called to other unformed lands, other Seekers, other Towers waiting to rise.

Before vanishing entirely, the Builder spoke: "The Tower is yours now. I have given you not just stone, but the knowledge of stone. When the next storm comes—and it will come—you will know how to stand."

The Seeker nodded, and the Builder passed into the space between summons, carrying only the memory of another structure raised against chaos, another small victory in the endless building of worlds.
